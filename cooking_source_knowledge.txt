Sample Source Knowledge about Cooking
Basics of Cooking
Cooking Techniques:

Baking: Cooking food by surrounding it with dry heat in an oven.
Boiling: Cooking food by immersing it in water at a high temperature.
Frying: Cooking food by immersing it in hot fat or oil.
Grilling: Cooking food on a grill over direct heat.
Steaming: Cooking food using steam, typically by placing it in a basket over boiling water.
Common Ingredients:

Spices: Such as salt, pepper, cumin, and turmeric.
Herbs: Like basil, parsley, rosemary, and thyme.
Proteins: Including chicken, beef, pork, and tofu.
Vegetables: Such as carrots, potatoes, onions, and peppers.
Grains: Including rice, quinoa, and barley.
Cooking Tips:

Always preheat your oven before baking.
Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature of meats.
Let cooked meat rest before slicing to retain juices.
Taste and season your food as you cook.
Safety in the Kitchen:

Wash hands and surfaces often to prevent cross-contamination.
Use separate cutting boards for raw meat and vegetables.
Cook foods to the proper temperature to kill harmful bacteria.
Store perishable foods in the refrigerator within two hours of cooking.
